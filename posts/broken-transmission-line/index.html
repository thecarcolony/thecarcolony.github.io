Introduction#

Welcome to Phillips Vision! In this episode of Andy’s Garage, we will be discussing the most effective method to repair a damaged transmission cooler line. There are various ways you can approach this issue, such as using hose with clamps or AM fittings with braided hose. However, in our expert opinion, utilizing an actual steel line with flared ends and compression fittings on both ends will provide the best long-term solution. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can ensure a successful repair and prevent future failures. So, let’s dive in!

Step-by-Step Guide to Repairing Damaged Transmission Lines#

Step 1: Assess the Damage#

Take a close look at the damaged transmission line. It might have been previously patched, but if the job was poorly executed, the line could still be compromised. To start the repair process, you will need to remove the bracket that secures the line in place. This will allow you better access to the damaged section that needs to be replaced.

Step 2: Cut Out the Damaged Section#

Using a regular pipe cutting tool, carefully cut out the damaged section of the transmission line. This will ensure a clean cut, making it easier to connect the new steel line using compression fittings. Make sure to cut precisely where the connection with the compression fitting will be made.

Step 3: Prepare the Line for Repair#

Before connecting the new steel line, remove any wire protective wrap that may be covering the line. Although this step can be a bit time-consuming, it is necessary to create a seamless connection between the new line and the compression fittings. Once the protective wrap is removed, proceed to the next step.

Step 4: Connect the Compression Fittings#

Attach the compression fittings to both ends of the new steel line. One end will connect to the remaining portion of the transmission line, while the other end will connect to the section you cut out. Ensure that the fittings are securely tightened for a leak-free repair.

Step 5: Double-Check and Reassemble#

Verify that all connections are securely tightened and there are no loose fittings. Once you have confirmed this, reattach the line to the brackets and ensure everything is properly aligned. Finally, give the repaired transmission line a thorough inspection for any signs of leaks or issues.

Benefits of Using Steel Lines with Compression Fittings#

Using steel lines with compression fittings for repairing damaged transmission lines offers several advantages:

  1. Durability: Unlike hoses and clamps, steel lines with compression fittings provide a more reliable and long-lasting solution. They can withstand high pressure and are less prone to deterioration over time.
  2. Leak-Free: Compression fittings create a tight seal, ensuring that there are no leaks in the repaired transmission line. This eliminates the need for frequent re-patching or repair.
  3. Professional Finish: The use of steel lines and compression fittings provides a more professional and aesthetically pleasing look. It gives your repair job a seamless appearance and enhances the overall performance of the transmission system.
  4. Minimal Future Maintenance: Once the damaged transmission line is repaired using steel lines and compression fittings, you can expect minimal maintenance. This means you can have peace of mind knowing that your vehicle’s transmission system will function optimally for an extended period.

Conclusion#

Repairing damaged transmission lines requires careful consideration and the right approach. By using steel lines with compression fittings, you can achieve a long-lasting and effective solution. This method ensures durability, leak-free operation, and a professional finish. Following our step-by-step guide will help you successfully repair the damaged transmission line in your vehicle.
