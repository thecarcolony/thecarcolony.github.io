When considering window tints for your vehicle, it’s important to be well-informed before heading to a window tint shop. In this article, we will discuss the key points to consider when choosing the right window tint for your car. From the different types of window tint films available to understanding VLT (Visible Light Transmission) and the benefits of ceramic films, we’ve got you covered.

1. Not all window tint films are the same#

There are various types of window tint films available, each with its own characteristics. The most affordable option is the dyed film, which consists of an adhesive layer, a dyed layer, and a polyester protective layer. While this option is a cost-effective choice, it typically lasts between two to five years and may fade or turn purple over time, especially if exposed to sunlight for extended periods.

2. Consider metallized layer films#

A step above dyed films is the metallized layer film, which contains both a metal layer and a dyed layer. This type of film offers better fading resistance and can help keep your car a little cooler. However, it tends to have a reflective layer, similar to a mirror effect. Chicago Auto Pros, however, does not carry this type of film, as they believe there are better performing films on the market.

3. Look for carbon and ceramic layer films#

Carbon and ceramic layer films are highly recommended by Chicago Auto Pros. These films are designed to block infrared heat and provide better performance and aesthetics. Carbon films have a layer of carbon, while ceramic films have a layer of ceramic. These films are effective in blocking infrared rays, which are responsible for the heat that enters your car through the windows. Compared to dyed films that block only around 20% of infrared rays, ceramic films can block up to 98%, resulting in a significantly cooler car.

4. Understanding Visible Light Transmission (VLT)#

VLT, or Visible Light Transmission, refers to the percentage of light that can pass through the window tint film. A lower VLT means less light can pass through, resulting in a darker tint. A five percent VLT, often referred to as limo tint, is very dark. On the other hand, a fifty percent VLT allows for more light transmission, creating a lighter tint. Chicago Auto Pros offers window tint films with various VLT options, ranging from five percent to eighty percent. They even have cars with different VLT percentages available for customers to experience and compare.

5. Window tint laws and regulations#

Before deciding on the right window tint for your car, it’s essential to be aware of the window tint laws in your area. Each state has its own regulations regarding the permissible level of tint darkness.
